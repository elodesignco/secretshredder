# Secret Shredder Voice System

## Brand promise
Secret Shredder is not productivity software. It is a tiny stage-managed ritual for harmless regret.

People come here to do three things:
1. admit a ridiculous thing
2. watch it get obliterated beautifully
3. send the result to a friend with "look at this stupid brilliant thing"

## Core vibe
- **Premium mischief** — polished, not cheap
- **Playfully dramatic** — theatrical, but still clean
- **Kind to the user** — we tease the moment, not the person
- **Internet-native** — quotable, clip-friendly, screenshot-friendly
- **Never naff** — if a line feels like novelty merch copy, cut it

## Personality sliders
- Funny: high
- Chaotic: medium
- Mean: low
- Clever: high
- Self-aware: high
- Trustworthy: high
- Therapy language: low
- Tech-builder language in public: near zero

## What we sound like
- A luxury machine with a sense of humour
- A smug little ceremony host
- The friend who says "that is horrific, put it in the shredder immediately"

## What we do NOT sound like
- Corporate SaaS
- Meme sludge / random chaos for its own sake
- Snarky or cruel
- Clinical mental-health support
- Builder notes leaking into public copy

## Messaging pillars
### 1. Confession without consequence
Users should feel they can drop a harmless cringe memory in and move on.

### 2. Destruction as entertainment
The destruction moment is the product, not an afterthought.

### 3. Shareable spectacle
If it does not look good in a screen recording, it is not finished.

### 4. Premium absurdity
The joke lands harder when the execution feels expensive.

## Copy rules
- Prefer short, quotable sentences.
- Use contrast: elegant words + silly premise.
- One clever line per block is enough.
- Do not stack wink-wink phrases in every sentence.
- Buttons must be direct, not overly clever.
- Badges can be witty, but never mistaken for controls.
- Safety copy should be plain, human, and immediate.

## Sentence patterns that fit
- "A tiny luxury machine for terrible little memories."
- "Some regrets deserve a normal delete button. Yours deserve theatre."
- "Type it. Pick a fate. Watch the evidence become confetti."

## Sentence patterns to avoid
- "Unleash the ultimate cringe obliteration experience"
- "Next-gen catharsis platform"
- "Pay. Confess. Heal." (too therapy-coded)
- Anything that sounds like merch copy on a novelty mug

## Word bank
### Favour
shred, vanish, obliterate, confess, feed the machine, fate, ritual, stage, spectacle, evidence, closure, ridiculous, premium, ceremonial, socially fatal, harmless, dramatic

### Use carefully
shame, guilt, sinful, exorcism, therapy, trauma

### Avoid
solution, platform, leverage, revolutionary, disruptive, users, pipeline, architecture, stack (in public)

## Humour guardrails
- Joke with the situation, not at vulnerable people.
- Keep the stakes small and recognisable.
- Harmless cringe beats heavy confession.
- Never encourage doxxing, revenge, threats, or real harm.

## CTA rules
- Primary CTA: plain and strong
- Secondary CTA: exploratory
- No mystery-meat button labels

Good:
- Join early access
- Try a demo shred
- Pick a shred style

Bad:
- Feed the beast
- Do the thing
- Enter the void

## Public promise
Public-facing pages should make three things obvious within seconds:
1. this is anonymous-ish / low-data by design
2. this is a fun ritual, not counselling
3. the animation/destruction experience is the star
